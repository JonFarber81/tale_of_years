# Character & dynasty system

Type: grilling
Status: resolved
Blocked by: 02, 03

## Question

How are named people, their lives, and their bloodlines modeled over an open-ended yearly sim?

Decide:

- **Lifecycle** — birth, aging, death (natural, violence, disease); how characters are generated vs. seeded from canon.
- **Race & lifespan** — must handle **elven immortality** and very-long lifespans (Dúnedain, dwarves, Maiar) alongside mortal Men, Hobbits, Orcs. How does aging/mortality differ by race, and how do immortals behave in a system built around generational turnover?
- **Dynasties & succession** — families, marriage, heirs, succession rules for realms (Gondor's Stewards, Rohan's kings, Durin's line, the Chieftains of the Dúnedain).
- **Traits & roles** — the minimal character attributes that drive behavior (leadership, martial skill, ambition, loyalty) and roles (ruler, heir, general, Ring-bearer).
- **Relationships** — kinship, allegiance, rivalry — enough to make history feel personal.

Consult `/domain-modeling`. Feeds war (generals), diplomacy (marriages), and the Ring (bearers).

## Answer

Characters are ordinary id-keyed records driven by a **data-tabled, per-race lifecycle** in tick phase 1; dynasties are an **emergent kinship graph**, not a stored entity; a **small trait vector + role enum** drives behaviour; relationships are **kinship id-fields plus a thin event-created sentiment layer**.

### 1. Lifecycle — birth, aging, death

- Phase 1 (`aging/births/deaths`) ages every character, then rolls **natural + disease death** against an age→annual-death-probability curve, and **births** on established couples via a fertility model — all off the single seeded RNG.
- **Violent death is not rolled here** — phase 5 (war) kills named characters directly. Phase 1 owns only natural/disease death.
- **Seed characters** (the ~2965 canon roster from research 03) are hand-authored records loaded at scenario start; everyone thereafter is generated by births. Unnamed population is a faction/economy aggregate (06/08), not individual records.

### 2. Race & lifespan — immortals via config, with light fading *(fork resolved)*

- A **`race` config table** gives each race `{mortality_kind, maturity_age, fertility_window, base_death_curve}`, with `mortality_kind ∈ {mortal, long_lived, immortal}`. Long-lived (Dúnedain, dwarves) use a stretched curve; same machinery, no special-case code.
- **Immortals (Elves, Maiar): phase 1 skips the natural-death roll entirely.** They leave play only via violence (phase 5), the Ring/Sauron (09), or —
- **Chosen: light fade & departure.** Elves carry a slowly-rising **weariness / sail-West drive**; over centuries individuals **depart** (tombstoned as `departed`, not `dead`), reusing the existing `alive=False` mechanism. This pairs with the Elf-realm **`withdrawing` posture** in ticket 06 (defend, seldom expand/muster), giving the canon fading-Third-Age feel. A deeper Ring-linked collapse of the Elf realms was **not** adopted for v1 — it stays in the magic/aftermath fog.

### 3. Dynasties & succession — emergent graph, no Dynasty entity

- **No separate `Dynasty`/`House` record.** A bloodline is a query over Character kinship edges (`parent_ids`, `spouse_id`); a realm's ruling line is simply whoever holds `faction.leader_id`. (The chronicle UI, 11, may cache a derived "House" view for display — never authoritative.)
- Each faction carries a **`succession_rule`** (config enum: `agnatic_primogeniture`, `elective`, `stewardship`, `dwarf_line_of_durin`, …). On a leader's death/departure a small succession step (phase-1/phase-2 seam) walks the kinship graph under that rule and emits a `succession` event.
- **Failed line / dead-out realm *(fork resolved — rule-driven mix)*:** the rule first tries kin, then elective/relative fallback; if that truly fails, the realm **fragments or is absorbed by the strongest bordering faction** via a conquest-like event; a faction reduced to zero territory is **tombstoned but its dormant claim persists**, keeping restoration (e.g. Arnor) reachable. The territory/absorption mechanics are 06's to run; 05 owns the heir-walk.
- Canon shapes via the rule, not scripts: Gondor = stewardship (an Isildur-heir may emergently press a claim), Rohan = agnatic, Durin's Folk = dwarf line, Dúnedain Chieftains = agnatic.

### 4. Traits & roles

- A **small fixed trait vector** of scalars — `leadership, martial, ambition, loyalty` (+ optional `wisdom/guile`) — sampled at generation from race + parentage with **mild heritability** (RNG jitter), so strong lines emerge as a tendency. War picks generals by `martial`+`leadership`; faction/diplomacy AI reads `ambition`/`loyalty`.
- **`role`** is the enum already on Character (02): `ruler, heir, general, ring_bearer, ranger, councillor, none`. 05 fixes the vocabulary and assignment rules.
- **Character prominence** (needed by 11's salience scoring) is derived from role + trait magnitude + title; 05 owns this field so the chronicle doesn't have to invent it.

### 5. Relationships — kinship + thin sentiment *(fork resolved)*

- **Structural kinship** stored as id fields (`parent_ids: list[int]`, `spouse_id`; siblings/descent derived by query) — authoritative, survives tombstoning, drives succession and the Ring's inheritance tendency.
- **Plus a thin sentiment layer:** a lightweight typed edge list `(from_id, to_id, type ∈ {rivalry, friendship, oath}, strength)`, created **only by events** (a betrayal in phase 3, a shared battle in phase 5) and read by diplomacy. **Edges exist only where an event made one** — no O(n²) matrix. Allegiance-to-a-faction stays `faction_id`; allegiance-to-a-person is an oath edge.

### The Bilbo→Frodo tendency (boundary with 09)

05 models the Baggins line as **ordinary kinship + fertility** and **must not encode "Frodo."** It only guarantees the structure exists so that *if* the line produces a nephew-heir ~2968, the Ring (ticket 09, biased by the canonicity parameter) can favour him. "Tendency, not script" lives at the 05↔09 seam.

### Seams & fog surfaced

- **05↔08 (marriage):** 08 decides *whether* a marriage happens (phase 3); 05 defines *what a marriage does* to the kinship graph and succession. Explicit seam for ticket 08.
- **05→06 (interregnum):** the territory side of a failed line (fragment/absorb/dormant-claim) is 06's to implement.
- **Fog:** trait-heritability strength is coupled to the canonicity parameter (ticket 10). Character-generation breadth (how many named non-rulers spawn/year) feeds the performance-at-scale and content-authoring fog — 05 sets the spawn rate, not the budget.
