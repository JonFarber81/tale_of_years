# Research: canonical state of Middle-earth in TA 2965

Type: research
Status: resolved

## Question

Compile the canonical starting state of NW Middle-earth as of **TA 2965**, accurate enough to seed the simulation, from high-trust Tolkien sources (LotR appendices, The Silmarillion, Unfinished Tales, reputable wikis cross-checked against primary text).

Gather:

- **Powers & realms** and who rules them in 2965: Gondor (Steward — Turgon, d. 2953; then Ecthelion II), Rohan (Thengel/Théoden era), Arnor's remnant peoples (Rangers of the North, Chieftain — Aragorn is ~34), the Shire, Rivendell (Elrond), Lórien (Galadriel/Celeborn), Mirkwood (Thranduil), Erebor & Dale (Dáin II, Bard's line), the Iron Hills, Isengard (Saruman), the Dúnedain, Bree.
- **Sauron's status in 2965** — Mordor being rebuilt, Barad-dûr (openly rebuilt 2951), the Nazgûl, Dol Guldur, Minas Morgul, orc/troll strength, when the Nazgûl reoccupy the relevant strongholds.
- **The One Ring** — where it is and who holds it in 2965 (Bilbo, in the Shire; Frodo not yet), and the canonical dates it moves (2941 found; 3001 to Frodo; 3018 leaves the Shire).
- **Key living characters** in 2965 with approximate birth years/ages/locations (Gandalf, Aragorn, Elrond, Galadriel, Saruman, Denethor, Théoden, Thranduil, Legolas, Gimli, Frodo/Bilbo, Dáin, etc.).
- **Off-map providers** — what's canonically known of Harad, Rhûn, Khand as Sauron's allies (troops, oliphaunts, corsairs of Umbar) — enough to model them as abstract providers.
- **Settlements & strongholds** to place on the map, with rough locations.

Deliverable: a cited markdown file at `.scratch/arda-history-v1/research/canonical-2965-state.md`. This feeds the seed for the character, faction, Ring, and Sauron tickets.

## Answer

Resolved. Findings written to [`research/canonical-2965-state.md`](../research/canonical-2965-state.md), covering: rulers of every realm in 2965 (Ecthelion II in Gondor, Thengel in Rohan, Aragorn age 34 ranging south as Thorongil, Dáin II at Erebor, Saruman fortifying Isengard); Sauron's status (openly back since 2951, Barad-dûr rebuilt, Nazgûl active, Mount Doom NOT yet erupting); the Ring quiescent with Bilbo (75) at Bag End; a character table with ages; a **not-yet-born** list (Frodo b.2968, Boromir, Faramir, Éomer, Éowyn, Sam, Merry, Pippin, Théodred — all after 2965); off-map providers (Haradrim+mûmakil, Easterlings, Variags, Corsairs of Umbar); and settlement placements. Key modeling takeaway: the Ring's canonical inheritance (Bilbo→Frodo) must be a *tendency*, not a guarantee, since Frodo isn't born yet at seed.
