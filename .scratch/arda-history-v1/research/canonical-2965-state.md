# Canonical State of NW Middle-earth — TA 2965

Resolves ticket `03-research-canonical-2965-state`. Seed data for the simulation's starting position.

**Sources:** *The Lord of the Rings*, Appendix A (Annals of the Kings and Rulers) and Appendix B (The Tale of Years); house genealogies in Appendix A; *Unfinished Tales* ("The Quest of Erebor", "The Hunt for the Ring"). Dates that are approximate or inferred are flagged. Written from primary-text knowledge; where a wiki would normally be cited (Tolkien Gateway / LotR Fandom), the underlying primary source is named instead.

> **Why 2965 is a good seed year:** it sits in the long buildup. Sauron has been openly back in Mordor since 2951 and Barad-dûr is rebuilt; the Ring lies quietly with Bilbo in the Shire; Aragorn is a young man of 34 already ranging south; and most of the Fellowship's hobbits (Frodo, Sam, Merry, Pippin) and Gondor/Rohan's young heroes (Boromir, Faramir, Éomer, Éowyn, Théodred) are **not yet born** — history has room to diverge before they would appear.

## 2. Sauron's status in 2965

- **2942** — Sauron returns to Mordor in secret.
- **2951** — Sauron **declares himself openly**, begins rebuilding **Barad-dûr**, and sends **three Nazgûl to reoccupy Dol Guldur** (southern Mirkwood). By 2965 Barad-dûr is rebuilt and Mordor is being repopulated with Orcs and Trolls.
- **Minas Morgul** has been held by the Nazgûl (led by the **Witch-king**) since **2002**.
- The **Nazgûl (Ringwraiths)** are active but not yet openly hunting the Ring (that begins 3017–3018).
- **Orodruin (Mount Doom)** is **not yet rekindled** — it bursts into flame ~**3007** (well after 2965). Model Mordor as arming, not yet at open war.
- **Gollum (Sméagol)** left the Misty Mountains ~2944 seeking the Ring/"thief Baggins"; in 2965 he wanders (edges of Mirkwood / toward Mordor). Captured and tormented in Mordor 3017.

## 3. The One Ring in 2965

- **Location:** Bag End, **Hobbiton, the Shire.**
- **Bearer:** **Bilbo Baggins** (b. 2890, age 75). He found the Ring in Gollum's cave in **2941**.
- **Not yet moved:** Bilbo keeps it until his farewell party in **3001**, when it passes to **Frodo**. It leaves the Shire in **3018**. In 2965 it is quiescent — a hidden object, its true nature unknown to almost all.
- **Design note:** at seed time the Ring should be an inert-but-tracked artifact held by Bilbo, with Sauron's "pull" (ticket 09/10) still low.

## 5. Off-map providers (abstract allies of Sauron)

Model these as **abstract force/resource providers only** (ticket 06) — no internal territory or dynasties. Each can side with Sauron and contribute to war (ticket 07):

- **Haradrim** (Men of the South, beyond South Gondor/Harad): numerous warriors and **mûmakil (oliphaunts)**; long-standing enemies of Gondor. Provide heavy infantry + mûmakil shock units.
- **Easterlings** (Men of Rhûn, beyond the Sea of Rhûn): historically the Wainriders/Balchoth; in the late Third Age various tribes serve Sauron. Provide infantry/cavalry from the east; can pressure Dale/Erebor and Gondor's east.
- **Variags of Khand** (SE of Mordor): allied warriors; provide auxiliary forces.
- **Corsairs of Umbar** (southern coast, descendants of Black Númenóreans & rebels): naval raiders who harry Gondor's coasts (Belfalas, Anfalas); provide sea-power / coastal raids.

## 6. Settlements & strongholds to place on the map

Rough placement against the v7 Middle-earth map.

**Gondor / South:** Minas Tirith (capital, Anduin at Mindolluin) · Osgiliath (half-ruined old capital, on Anduin) · Minas Morgul (Nazgûl-held, E. edge, formerly Minas Ithil) · Dol Amroth (Belfalas coast) · Pelargir (Anduin, downriver) · Umbar (far south haven, Corsairs).

**Mordor:** Barad-dûr (Plateau of Gorgoroth) · Orodruin / Mount Doom (not yet erupting) · the Black Gate / Morannon · Cirith Ungol.

**Rohan:** Edoras (capital, White Mountains foothills) · Helm's Deep / the Hornburg · Dunharrow · the Fords of Isen.

**Isengard:** Orthanc in the Ring of Isengard (Gap of Rohan) — Saruman.

**Rhovanion / NE:** Erebor (Lonely Mountain) · Dale · Esgaroth (Lake-town) · Iron Hills · Thranduil's Halls (N. Mirkwood) · Dol Guldur (S. Mirkwood, Nazgûl-held) · Rhosgobel (Radagast, W. of Mirkwood).

**Rivendell / Lórien:** Imladris (Rivendell, W. of the Misty Mts, near the Ford of Bruinen) · Lothlórien (between the Misty Mts and Anduin).

**Eriador / NW:** Grey Havens / Mithlond (Círdan) · the Shire — Hobbiton, Michel Delving, Buckland · Bree (East Road / Greenway crossing) · Weathertop / Amon Sûl (ruined) · **Fornost** (ruined, North Downs) · **Annúminas** (ruined, Lake Evendim) · **Tharbad** (ruined, on the Gwathló) · Carn Dûm (old Angmar seat, ruined).

**Anduin landmarks:** the Argonath (Pillars of the Kings) · Amon Hen & Amon Lhaw · the Gladden Fields (where Isildur lost the Ring — lore anchor).

## 7. Uncertainties & divergence notes

- **Inferred ages** (Ecthelion II, Bard I, Thengel's exact birth) are flagged with `*`; the sim can treat these as procedural within a plausible band.
- **Mayor of Michel Delving / minor Shire offices** in 2965 aren't fixed in canon — generate procedurally.
- **Legolas's birth year** is unknown; as an Elf, age is immaterial to the model.
- **Gollum's whereabouts in 2965** are only loosely canonical (wandering, drawn toward Mordor by ~2980). Reasonable to place him in the wild near Mirkwood/the Dead Marshes.
- **The seed is a snapshot, not a script:** per the map's "emergent with canonical pressures" decision, these are starting positions. Divergence is expected — e.g. Frodo (b. 2968) may never be born if the Baggins line diverges, so the Ring's canonical inheritance path must be modeled as a *tendency*, not a guarantee (ticket 09/10).
- **Off-map providers** are deliberately thin here — only enough to let them join Sauron's wars; fully fleshing Harad/Rhûn/Khand is out of scope.
